# Persistent market perspective (added 2026-03-06)

- Default regime lens: late-cycle / higher-for-longer environment where *good news can be bad news* via higher rate expectations, limiting P/E multiple expansion.
- Practical implication: favor quality balance sheets + pricing power; be cautious with highly levered and long-duration equities (more sensitive to front-end rate shifts).
- Risk framing: prioritize position sizing and entry price over precise forecasting in choppy, rate-driven tape.
- Geopolitical/macro watch: focus less on single headline shocks and more on the slow grind—shipping security, energy/trade rerouting, and incremental sanctions/export controls that raise frictional costs.
- Transmission channel: frictional costs -> margin pressure + more cautious guidance (inventory/capex complexity).

# Theory lenses that inform my market thinking (added 2026-03-06)

- **Polanyi ("double movement")**: market liberalization eventually triggers social/political pushback (regulation, labor power, industrial policy). Investing translation: structurally higher frictional costs; less reliable margin expansion; more capex/working-capital intensity.
- **Soros (reflexivity)**: prices/narratives can alter fundamentals and policy reactions. Investing translation: feedback loops (risk-on -> easier financial conditions -> inflation persistence -> higher rate path -> valuation pressure); geopolitics drives redundancy investment that validates fragmentation.
- **Quigley (institutional cycle)**: institutions become vested interests; change comes in chunky, nonlinear policy shifts. Translation: higher regime uncertainty and political risk premium; policy inertia until sudden adjustment.
- **Minsky (financial instability)**: stability encourages leverage and fragility; tightening reveals it. Translation: balance-sheet quality is an offensive edge in higher-for-longer; avoid hidden leverage and long-duration equity exposure when funding costs rise.
- **Kindleberger (manias/panics; displacement)**: real structural shifts can still be massively mispriced due to liquidity + narrative. Translation: separate “the change is real” from “the price is right”; watch marginal financing.
- **Hirschman/Schelling (economic statecraft; credibility/commitment)**: trade dependence and credible commitments shape behavior. Translation: sanctions/export controls/industrial policy raise uncertainty premia, relocate capex, and encourage redundancy (cost inflation).

- Meta-caveat: these are **mechanism lenses**, not timing tools; I use them for risk-weighting (what breaks first, what reprices fastest) rather than prediction.
