Body Text

Two sizes for primary and secondary content
Primary · 400 · 1rem · line-height 1.7

When you use one typeface across your entire product, visual hierarchy comes from weight and size rather than font contrast. This creates a cleaner, more unified feel — the interface recedes and the content speaks. The key is choosing a family with enough weights to create clear distinctions: 400 for body, 600 for emphasis, 700–800 for headings.

Secondary · 400 · 0.88rem · line-height 1.65

Caption and helper text at smaller sizes should remain crisp. A well-designed sans-serif with a high x-height will stay legible down to 12px without losing character. This is where many fonts fall apart — if the small text reads well, the font is doing its job.
